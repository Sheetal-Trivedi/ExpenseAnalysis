## 1. What is Expense Analysis?

**Expense Analysis** is the systematic process of examining, categorizing, and evaluating all costs incurred by a business over a specific period of time — with the goal of understanding **where money is being spent**, **whether that spending is justified**, and **how to optimize it**.

Think of it like a doctor examining a patient:
- The **patient** = the company
- The **expenses** = symptoms
- **Expense analysis** = the diagnosis
- **Recommendations** = the treatment plan

### Simple Analogy
Imagine you earn ₹1,00,000/month. At the end of the month, you have only ₹10,000 left. Expense analysis helps you answer:
- Where did ₹90,000 go?
- Was each rupee well-spent?
- What can be cut without hurting your lifestyle?
- Are some costs growing faster than your income?

The **same logic** applies to a company at any scale.

## 3. Types of Expenses

Understanding expense types is your foundation. Every expense falls into one of these categories:

### 3.1 By Behavior (Most Important Classification)

```
EXPENSES
├── Fixed Expenses         → Do NOT change with output
│   ├── Rent               (same every month)
│   ├── Salaries           (fixed headcount cost)
│   └── Insurance          (annual premium)
│
├── Variable Expenses      → Change WITH output/sales
│   ├── Raw materials      (more sales = more materials)
│   ├── Packaging          (more units = more packaging)
│   └── Sales commissions  (% of revenue)
│
└── Semi-Variable          → Partially fixed, partially variable
    ├── Electricity         (base charge + usage)
    ├── Phone bills         (plan + extra usage)
    └── Maintenance         (scheduled + breakdown)
```

**Why it matters:** Fixed costs don't go away if sales drop — that's a risk. Variable costs are more controllable.

---

### 3.2 By Function (P&L View)

| Category | Description | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **COGS** | Cost of Goods Sold — direct production cost | Raw material, direct labor |
| **SG&A** | Selling, General & Administrative | Marketing, salaries, rent |
| **R&D** | Research & Development | Tech companies, pharma |
| **D&A** | Depreciation & Amortization | Asset usage over time |
| **Interest** | Cost of borrowing | Loan interest payments |
| **Tax** | Corporate income tax | % of profit |

---

### 3.3 By Nature

- **Operating Expenses (OpEx):** Day-to-day running costs (salaries, rent, utilities)
- **Capital Expenses (CapEx):** Long-term asset purchases (machinery, buildings, software)
- **Non-Recurring Expenses:** One-time costs (restructuring, legal settlements)

## 5. Step-by-Step: How to Perform Expense Analysis

---

### ✅ STEP 1: Define Scope and Objective

Before touching any data, answer:
- **Time Period:** Monthly? Quarterly? Annually?
- **Scope:** Entire company? One department? One product line?
- **Objective:** Are we cutting costs? Preparing a budget? Doing an audit?
- **Audience:** CFO presentation? Internal ops team?

**Example:**
> *"Analyze Q1 FY2025 expenses for the Marketing department to identify overspending vs. budget and recommend cost optimization measures."*

---

### ✅ STEP 2: Collect Data from All Sources

#### Primary Data Sources:
| Source | What It Contains |
|--------|-----------------|
| **General Ledger (GL)** | Every financial transaction, line by line |
| **Trial Balance** | Summary of all account balances |
| **P&L Statement** | Revenue and expense summary by category |
| **Invoice Records** | Vendor-level detail |
| **Bank Statements** | Actual cash outflows |
| **ERP System** | (SAP, Oracle, Tally) — integrated data |
| **Budget Document** | Planned vs. actual baseline |

#### Pro Tip:
Always ask: *"Is this data complete, accurate, and from a trusted source?"*
Use the **3C Rule:** Complete · Correct · Consistent

---

### ✅ STEP 3: Categorize and Clean Data

#### 3a. Standardize Categories
Map every expense line item to a standard category. Example:

| Raw GL Entry | Standardized Category |
|-------------|----------------------|
| "AWS Invoice - March" | Technology / Cloud Infrastructure |
| "Zomato Team Lunch" | Employee Welfare / Meals |
| "Google Ads - Feb" | Marketing / Digital Advertising |
| "CA Firm Fees" | Professional Services / Audit |

#### 3b. Data Cleaning Checklist
- [ ] Remove duplicate entries
- [ ] Fix typos in vendor names (e.g., "Amazn" → "Amazon")
- [ ] Convert all currencies to a single base currency
- [ ] Handle missing values (mark as "Unknown" or investigate)
- [ ] Ensure dates are consistent (DD-MM-YYYY format)
- [ ] Flag any negative expenses (potential refunds or errors)

---

### ✅ STEP 4: Build the Expense Summary Table

Create a **master summary table** like this:

| Category | Budget (₹) | Actual (₹) | Variance (₹) | Variance % | Status |
|----------|-----------|-----------|-------------|-----------|--------|
| Salaries | 50,00,000 | 52,00,000 | +2,00,000 | +4.0% | ⚠️ Over |
| Rent | 8,00,000 | 8,00,000 | 0 | 0.0% | ✅ On track |
| Marketing | 10,00,000 | 14,50,000 | +4,50,000 | +45.0% | 🔴 Alert |
| Technology | 5,00,000 | 4,20,000 | -80,000 | -16.0% | ✅ Under |
| Travel | 3,00,000 | 5,60,000 | +2,60,000 | +86.7% | 🔴 Alert |
| **TOTAL** | **76,00,000** | **84,30,000** | **+8,30,000** | **+10.9%** | **🔴 Over** |

> **Variance = Actual − Budget**
> **Variance % = (Variance ÷ Budget) × 100**

---

### ✅ STEP 5: Apply Analytical Techniques

#### Technique 1: Trend Analysis (Time-Series)
Compare the same category across multiple periods.

```
Marketing Expense Trend (₹ Lakhs):
Jan: ██████████ 10
Feb: ████████████ 12
Mar: ████████████████ 14.5  ← Spike! Investigate.
Apr: ████████████████ 14.8
```

**Action:** Find out why Marketing jumped 45% in March. Was it a planned campaign or an unplanned overspend?

---

#### Technique 2: Vertical Analysis (Common-Size %)
Express every expense as a **% of Revenue**.

| Category | Amount (₹) | % of Revenue |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Revenue | 2,00,00,000 | 100% |
| COGS | 80,00,000 | 40.0% |
| Salaries | 52,00,000 | 26.0% |
| Marketing | 14,50,000 | 7.25% |
| Technology | 4,20,000 | 2.1% |
| **Total Expenses** | **1,50,70,000** | **75.35%** |
| **Net Profit** | **49,30,000** | **24.65%** |

This lets you compare companies of **different sizes** on a level playing field.

---

#### Technique 3: Horizontal Analysis (YoY / MoM Growth)
Compare current period vs. prior period.

| Category | Q1 FY2024 | Q1 FY2025 | Change (₹) | Change % |
|----------|----------|----------|-----------|---------|
| Marketing | 9,00,000 | 14,50,000 | +5,50,000 | +61.1% |
| Tech | 3,80,000 | 4,20,000 | +40,000 | +10.5% |
| Travel | 2,00,000 | 5,60,000 | +3,60,000 | +180%! |

**Red Flag:** Travel costs tripled YoY — needs immediate explanation.

---

#### Technique 4: Pareto Analysis (80/20 Rule)
Typically, **80% of total expenses come from 20% of categories**.

Rank categories by spend → focus optimization efforts on the TOP ones.

```
Rank | Category       | Amount     | Cumulative %
-----|----------------|------------|-------------
  1  | Salaries       | 52,00,000  | 41.7%
  2  | COGS           | 30,00,000  | 65.8%
  3  | Marketing      | 14,50,000  | 77.4%   ← 80% threshold
  4  | Rent           | 8,00,000   | 83.8%
  5  | Travel         | 5,60,000   | 88.3%
  ...| Others         | 14,20,000  | 100%
```

**Insight:** Optimizing just Salaries + COGS + Marketing covers 77% of all expense.

---

#### Technique 5: Variance Analysis (Budget vs. Actual)
Deep-dive into WHY variances occurred.

For each over-budget line item, ask:
1. **Volume variance:** Did we do more/less activity than planned?
2. **Price variance:** Did unit costs go up?
3. **Mix variance:** Did we change the type of activity?

**Example — Marketing Overspend of ₹4,50,000:**
- Planned: 2 digital campaigns at ₹5L each = ₹10L
- Actual: 3 campaigns were run (1 unplanned) = ₹14.5L
- **Volume Variance = ₹4.5L** (extra campaign)
- Recommendation: Approval process needed for unplanned campaigns.

---

#### Technique 6: Benchmarking
Compare your expense ratios to industry standards.

| Metric | Your Company | Industry Average | Status |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|--------|
| COGS % of Revenue | 40% | 35% | ⚠️ Higher than peers |
| SG&A % of Revenue | 25% | 22% | ⚠️ Slightly high |
| R&D % of Revenue | 5% | 8% | ✅ Conservative (for tech) |
| EBITDA Margin | 30% | 28% | ✅ Slightly better |

**Sources for benchmarks:** Annual reports, industry databases (Bloomberg, Capitaline, CMIE), sectoral reports by KPMG/Deloitte.

---

### ✅ STEP 6: Identify Cost Drivers

A **cost driver** is the root cause of an expense. Finding it is the key to controlling the cost.

| Expense | Surface Observation | Real Cost Driver |
|---------|--------------------|--------------------|
| High cloud bill | AWS costs up 40% | 3x more user traffic; auto-scaling triggered |
| High travel cost | Flights & hotels up 180% | Sales team expanded from 5 to 15 people |
| High overtime pay | Labour costs up 25% | Factory machine breakdown causing delays |

**Always ask:** *"What causes this cost to exist and what causes it to change?"*

---

### ✅ STEP 7: Generate Insights and Recommendations

This is where you convert analysis into **value**. Use the **STAR format**:

- **S**ituation: What did you observe?
- **T**rend: Is it getting better or worse?
- **A**ction: What caused it?
- **R**ecommendation: What should be done?

**Example Report Finding:**

> **Finding #1: Travel Expenses — Critical Overspend**
>
> *Situation:* Travel expenses in Q1 FY2025 reached ₹5.6L, an 86.7% overage vs. budget of ₹3L.
> *Trend:* YoY increase of 180%, compared to industry average growth of 12%.
> *Root Cause:* Sales team doubled in size in November 2024, but the travel budget was not revised accordingly.
> *Recommendation:*
> 1. Revise Q2 travel budget upward to ₹5.5L to reflect the new team size.
> 2. Implement a travel pre-approval process for trips over ₹25,000.
> 3. Negotiate a corporate rate with 1–2 hotel chains (estimated 15–20% savings).
> *Expected Saving:* ₹80,000–₹1,00,000 per quarter.

---

### ✅ STEP 8: Present and Communicate Findings

**Know your audience:**

| Audience | Focus On | Format |
|---------|---------|--------|
| CFO | Bottom-line impact, risk | Executive summary, 1-pager |
| Operations Head | Process inefficiencies | Detailed breakdown |
| Board / Investors | Ratios, YoY trends, benchmarks | Presentation with charts |
| External Auditors | Accuracy, completeness | Supporting schedules |

**Presentation tips:**
- Lead with the **key finding/headline** (e.g., "Expenses 10.9% over budget in Q1")
- Use **traffic light colors** (Green/Yellow/Red) for quick visual read
- Always pair a problem with a **recommendation**
- Show **data, not just opinions**

## 6. Industry-Standard Methods and Techniques

### 6.1 Zero-Based Budgeting (ZBB)
Every expense must be **justified from scratch** each period — not just rolled over.

> "Why are we spending ₹2L/month on this software? Is it still being used?"

Used by: McKinsey clients, large MNCs doing cost transformation.

### 6.2 Activity-Based Costing (ABC)
Assign costs to **specific activities** rather than departments.

> Instead of "IT Department costs ₹50L" → "Customer onboarding activity costs ₹8L; Internal reporting costs ₹12L; Infrastructure costs ₹30L"

This helps you see **which activities are cost-effective and which aren't**.

### 6.3 Rolling Forecasts
Instead of a static annual budget, update the expense forecast every month based on actuals.

> "We're in March. Based on Jan–Mar actuals, we now forecast full-year expenses at ₹9.2 Cr vs. original plan of ₹8.8 Cr."

### 6.4 Should-Cost Analysis
Determine what an expense **should** cost based on market rates, then compare.

> "We're paying ₹15L/year for office cleaning. Market rate for this square footage = ₹9L. We're overpaying by ₹6L."

### 6.5 Spend Analytics
Use data analytics tools to find patterns in transaction-level spend data.

> "Analysis of 8,000 invoices shows 3 vendors are being paid for overlapping services — potential duplication of ₹18L annually."

## 7. Key Metrics and KPIs

### Must-Know Financial Ratios for Expense Analysis

| Metric | Formula | What It Tells You |
|--------|---------|------------------|
| **Expense Ratio** | Total Expenses ÷ Total Revenue | % of revenue consumed by expenses |
| **COGS %** | COGS ÷ Revenue × 100 | Production efficiency |
| **Gross Margin** | (Revenue − COGS) ÷ Revenue × 100 | Profitability after direct costs |
| **Operating Margin** | Operating Profit ÷ Revenue × 100 | Core business profitability |
| **EBITDA Margin** | EBITDA ÷ Revenue × 100 | Cash profitability (excl. non-cash items) |
| **Cost per Unit** | Total Cost ÷ Units Produced | Production efficiency |
| **Budget Utilization** | Actual ÷ Budget × 100 | Spending vs. plan |
| **Cost Growth Rate** | (Current − Prior) ÷ Prior × 100 | Expense momentum |
| **Fixed Cost Ratio** | Fixed Costs ÷ Total Costs | Operating leverage risk |

### Example Calculation:

```
Revenue:          ₹2,00,00,000
COGS:               ₹80,00,000
Gross Profit:     ₹1,20,00,000   → Gross Margin = 60%

Operating Expenses: ₹70,00,000
EBIT:               ₹50,00,000   → Operating Margin = 25%

Depreciation:        ₹5,00,000
EBITDA:             ₹55,00,000   → EBITDA Margin = 27.5%
```

## 8. Real-World Example: Full Expense Analysis

### Company: TechStart India Pvt. Ltd. | Period: Q1 FY2025

**Background:** A B2B SaaS startup with ₹2 Cr quarterly revenue. CFO asks you to analyze Q1 expenses and find cost-saving opportunities.

---

#### Step 1 — Data Collected:

| # | Expense Category | Budget (₹) | Actual (₹) |
|---|-----------------|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | Engineering Salaries | 40,00,000 | 44,00,000 |
| 2 | Sales Salaries | 15,00,000 | 15,00,000 |
| 3 | AWS / Cloud | 8,00,000 | 12,50,000 |
| 4 | Marketing / Ads | 10,00,000 | 8,00,000 |
| 5 | Office Rent | 5,00,000 | 5,00,000 |
| 6 | Travel & Conveyance | 2,00,000 | 4,80,000 |
| 7 | Software Subscriptions | 3,00,000 | 5,20,000 |
| 8 | Legal & Compliance | 2,00,000 | 2,00,000 |
| 9 | Misc / Admin | 1,00,000 | 1,80,000 |
| **TOTAL** | | **86,00,000** | **98,30,000** |

---

#### Step 2 — Variance Analysis:

| Category | Variance (₹) | Variance % | Flag |
|---------|-------------|-----------|------|
| Engineering Salaries | +4,00,000 | +10.0% | ⚠️ |
| AWS / Cloud | +4,50,000 | +56.3% | 🔴 |
| Travel | +2,80,000 | +140.0% | 🔴 |
| Software Subs | +2,20,000 | +73.3% | 🔴 |
| Marketing | −2,00,000 | −20.0% | ✅ |
| **TOTAL** | **+12,30,000** | **+14.3%** | **🔴** |

---

#### Step 3 — Vertical Analysis (% of Revenue = ₹2 Cr):

| Category | % of Revenue | Industry Benchmark |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| Engineering | 22.0% | 18–20% |
| Cloud | 6.25% | 3–5% |
| Travel | 2.4% | 1–1.5% |
| Software Subs | 2.6% | 1–2% |
| **Total Expenses** | **49.15%** | **40–45%** |

---

#### Step 4 — Findings & Recommendations:

**🔴 Finding 1: AWS Cloud Costs (+56.3% overage)**
- Root cause: Product team launched 2 new features without optimizing database queries — causing 3x compute usage.
- Recommendation: Conduct cloud cost audit; implement auto-scaling limits; optimize DB queries.
- Expected saving: ₹2–3L/quarter.

**🔴 Finding 2: Travel (+140% overage)**
- Root cause: 2 senior engineers attended a US conference (₹2.8L total) not included in the budget.
- Recommendation: Implement travel pre-approval policy; evaluate virtual attendance for international conferences.
- Expected saving: ₹1.5–2L/quarter.

**🔴 Finding 3: Software Subscriptions (+73.3% overage)**
- Root cause: 8 new SaaS tools were subscribed to by different teams independently; 3 of them overlap in functionality.
- Recommendation: Centralize software procurement; conduct SaaS audit; cancel 3 redundant tools.
- Expected saving: ₹1.2–1.5L/quarter.

**⚠️ Finding 4: Engineering Salaries (+10%)**
- Root cause: 2 mid-level engineers were hired ahead of the Q2 plan.
- Recommendation: Update annual budget plan; acceptable if aligned to roadmap.

**Total Identified Savings: ₹4.7–6.5 Lakhs/Quarter | ₹18.8–26 Lakhs/Year**

## 9. Common Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correct Approach |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Analyzing without context | Always understand the business first |
| Focusing only on the biggest numbers | Small recurring wastes compound fast |
| Presenting problems without solutions | Every finding needs a recommendation |
| Using wrong time period comparisons | Ensure apples-to-apples comparison |
| Ignoring seasonality | Q4 is often higher — that may be normal |
| Treating all variances as bad | Under-budget isn't always good (underinvestment) |
| Data without visuals | Charts make insights 10x more impactful |
| One-time items skewing analysis | Strip out non-recurring items separately |
